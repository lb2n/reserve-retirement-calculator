Reserve Retirement Resources

Official Links, Legislation & Reference Materials

This page provides links to authoritative sources for U.S. military reserve and National Guard retirement information. Always verify important decisions with official sources and your servicing personnel office.

Links open in new tabs and go to official government (.gov/.mil) websites when possible.

üìú Key Legislation (NDAA)

The National Defense Authorization Acts that established and modified reserve early retirement provisions:

Law Section Description Effective
NDAA 2008 (P.L. 110-181) Section 647 Established early retirement for qualifying active duty service (90 days = 3 months reduction, minimum age 50) Jan 28, 2008
NDAA 2010 (P.L. 111-84) Section 647 Added 12304b (preplanned missions) as qualifying service Oct 28, 2009
NDAA 2012 (P.L. 112-81) Section 504 Extended 12304b provisions and clarified qualifying service Dec 31, 2011
NDAA 2015 (P.L. 113-291) Section 621 Allowed 90-day periods to span consecutive fiscal years (previously had to be within same FY) Oct 1, 2014 (FY2015)
NDAA 2017 (P.L. 114-328) Section 514 Further clarifications to reserve retirement provisions Dec 23, 2016

Presidential authority to order Ready Reserve members to active duty during national emergency. Qualifies for early retirement.

Title 10

10 U.S.C. ¬ß 12304 - Selected Reserve Order to Active Duty

Authority for contingency operations. Includes 12304, 12304a (CONUS emergencies), and 12304b (preplanned missions).

32 U.S.C. ¬ß 502(f) - National Guard Duty

Title 32 duty for homeland defense when federally funded. Qualifies for early retirement when supporting a national emergency.

How reserve retirement pay is calculated using the points system and High-36 or BRS formulas.
